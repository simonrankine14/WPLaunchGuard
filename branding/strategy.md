# Baseline Brand Strategy

## Core Position
Baseline is a website QA platform for teams that need a clear standard before anything goes live.

It leads with technical credibility, then supports that story with migration confidence, client-ready reporting, and dependable sign-off.

## Positioning Statement
Baseline helps web teams, agencies, and delivery partners test website changes properly, confirm what works, and share clear evidence before release.

## Brand Promise
Set the standard before release.

## Audience
- Agencies managing QA, sign-off, and handover across multiple sites
- Internal web teams shipping redesigns, relaunches, and iterative website changes
- Delivery leads who need a trustworthy report, not a noisy testing dashboard

## Messaging Pillars
### 1. Clear Standard
Baseline gives teams a reference point for quality so change can be measured, checked, and signed off.

### 2. Trusted Evidence
Baseline turns testing into clear confirmation: what passed, what changed, what needs fixing, and what is ready.

### 3. Calm Delivery
Baseline helps teams move into release with composure, replacing guesswork with dependable evidence and readable reports.

## Category Language
- Primary category: Website QA platform
- Secondary proof points: release validation, migration confidence, client-safe reporting
- Avoid leading with: scanner, guard, cyber-security language, or WordPress-only framing

## Personality
- Technical
- Steady
- Measured
- Dependable
- Unshowy

## Tone Of Voice
- Plain English
- Precise, not dramatic
- Confident without swagger
- Evidence-first
- Professional and calm

## Descriptor Options
- Website QA with a clear standard.
- Website QA for confident sign-off.
- A clearer standard for website release checks.

## Proof The Product Already Supports
- Structured QA runs across pages, devices, and journeys
- HTML, PDF, and spreadsheet reporting for client and team use
- Visual comparison workflows
- Client-safe and developer-facing report modes
- White-label options for agencies

## Strategic Guardrails
- Technical without theatre
- Trustworthy but not bureaucratic
- Product-led, not plugin-led
- Wordmark-first identity with a minimal icon system
- Untitled Sans as the typographic anchor

## Recommended Direction
Use `Baseline` as the single public-facing master brand.

Reasoning:
- It is immediately legible and strongly aligned with QA, standards, testing, and sign-off.
- It feels technical and credible without sounding aggressive or security-led.
- It gives the product a calm, software-grade identity that can extend beyond WordPress.
